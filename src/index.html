If you're looking for an old-fashioned "do not stir" style of blackberry cobbler, you’ve come to the right place. The magic of this recipe lies in the layering technique: as it bakes, the batter rises to the top, creating a crisp crust that pairs perfectly with the sweet and slightly tart blackberries below.
Old-Fashioned
Blackberry Cobbler
By: Ann Taylor Pittman
That tastes like home.
Active Time: 12 mins
Total Time: 52 mins
Servings: 8
Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) salted butter, cut into 8 pieces
  • 5 cups fresh blackberries
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/2 tsp. table salt, divided
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (about 4 1/4 oz.)
  • 2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • Vanilla ice cream (for serving)
  1. Prepare the oven and dish:
Preheat oven to 375°F. Place butter in a 13 x 9-inch baking dish and set it in the oven as it heats.
While the oven heats, toss blackberries with 1/2 cup sugar and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a large bowl. Let stand for 10 minutes.
  1. Make the blackberry mixture:
Whisk together flour, remaining 1 cup sugar, baking powder, and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a medium bowl. Add milk and vanilla, and whisk until smooth.
  1. Prepare the batter:
Carefully remove the baking dish from the oven once the butter is melted. Pour the batter evenly over the butter. Do not stir. Scatter the blackberry mixture (including any juices) over the batter.
  1. Assemble the cobbler:
Bake at 375°F for 40-45 minutes until the topping is golden brown and the fruit mixture is bubbly. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream.
