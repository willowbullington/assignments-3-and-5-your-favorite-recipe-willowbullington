Old-Fashioned
Blackberry Cobbler
By: Ann Taylor Pittman
That tastes like home.
Active Time:12 mins
Total Time: 52 mins
Servings: 8
Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) salted butter, cut into 8 pieces
  • 5 cups fresh blackberries
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/2 tsp. table salt, divided
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (about 4 1/4 oz.)
  • 2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • Vanilla ice cream (for serving)3
  1. Prepare the oven and dish:
Preheat oven to 375°F. Place butter in a 13 x 9–inch baking dish and set it in the oven as it heats.
While the oven heats, toss blackberries with 1/2 cup sugar and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a large bowl. Let stand for 10 minutes.
  1. Make the blackberry mixture:
Whisk together flour, remaining 1 cup sugar, baking powder, and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a medium bowl. Add milk and vanilla, and whisk until smooth.
  1. Prepare the batter:
Carefully remove the baking dish from the oven once the butter is melted. Pour the batter evenly over the butter. Do not stir. Scatter the blackberry mixture (including any juices) over the batter.
  1. Assemble the cobbler:
Bake at 375°F for 40-45 minutes until the topping is golden brown and the fruit mixture is bubbly. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream.

Old-Fashioned
Blackberry Cobbler
The magic of this recipe lies in the layering technique: as it bakes, the batter rises to the top, creating a crisp crust that pairs perfectly with the sweet and slightly tart blackberries below. The no-stirring instructions are important to ensure the butter moves to the outer edges for a perfectly baked crust.
The secret to this cobbler is the balance between tart blackberries and a subtly sweet, soft crust. Unlike pies that require a lot of crust prep, this cobbler features a pourable batter, making it quick to assemble. The butter melts directly in the pan while the oven preheats, which allows the batter to rise up around the blackberries as it bakes, creating a beautiful golden topping.
It's a perfect dessert for blackberry season but can also be made year-round with frozen berries.


The no-stirring instructions are important: You want the butter to be pushed to the outer edges when you top it with the batter so the crust bakes perfectly.
Helpful Tips:

  • Blackberries: Fresh, plump blackberries work best for this recipe. If they’re out of season, frozen blackberries can be used, but thaw them before use and drain any excess liquid.

  • Butter: Salted butter adds an extra layer of flavor to the cobbler, balancing the sweetness of the berries and batter. For an even richer flavor, you can experiment with brown butter.

  • Vanilla: A teaspoon of vanilla adds warmth and enhances the flavor of the fruit, making the dessert even more aromatic and inviting.
